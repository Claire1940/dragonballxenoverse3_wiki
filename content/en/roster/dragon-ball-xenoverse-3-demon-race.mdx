<Section icon={Swords} title="Overhauled RPG Systems and Stats">

The progression system in Dragon Ball Xenoverse 3 represents a significant departure from its predecessor. The developers have opted for a more streamlined approach to character growth, focusing on five core attributes. This simplification aims to make the competitive landscape more accessible while maintaining depth through the new skill cooldown system.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Simplified Attributes">
    - **HP**: Overall health pool.
    - **Strike Attack/Defense**: Governs physical melee effectiveness.
    - **Blast Attack/Defense**: Governs energy-based skill power.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Resource Management">
    - **Centralized Ki**: A single bar for all actions.
    - **No Stamina Stat**: Defensive moves now pull from the Ki pool.
    - **Homogenized Bars**: Most characters start with five Ki bars.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

The removal of Ki and Stamina as levelable stats ensures that the playing field is more even in terms of resource availability. Every character, including bosses like Broly (Super), manages a similar five-bar resource system. This shifts the focus from "stat padding" to "resource management," where knowing when to dash or use a defensive break is more critical than having a massive stamina pool.

| Stat Name | Function | Impact on Gameplay |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **HP** | Maximum Health | Determines survivability in high-level boss raids |
| **Strike Attack** | Melee Damage | Increases the power of square/triangle combos |
| **Strike Defense** | Melee Mitigation | Reduces damage taken from physical assaults |
| **Blast Attack** | Skill Damage | Enhances Kamehameha and other energy supers |
| **Blast Defense** | Skill Mitigation | Vital for surviving ultimate attacks like Heat Dome |

<Callout type="warning" title="Critical Change">
Because skills no longer cost Ki but are instead on cooldown, you cannot spam ultimates. You must wait for the timer to reset, making every Super Kamehameha a high-stakes decision.

<Section icon={Map} title="Combat Mechanics: Soul Assist and Soul Switch">

Two brand-new mechanics define the flow of battle in the 2026 sequel: **Soul Assist** and **Soul Switch**. These systems replace the older "Escape" abilities and modify how players interact with their custom avatars. For those interested in the **dragon ball xenoverse 3 demon race**, these mechanics could offer unique visual flares or specific tactical advantages.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Soul Assist Integration">
    Players can equip two "Soul Assists" from legendary Z-Warriors or villains. These function as summons that provide a quick offensive or defensive burst without consuming Ki.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Soul Switch Activation">
    Located where the awakening slot used to be, Soul Switch allows you to transform into a mentor character (like Future Trunks) for a limited duration (approx. 40-60 seconds).
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Move Set Overwrite">
    While Soul Switched, your melee combos, supers, and ultimates are replaced by the chosen mentor's kit, offering a temporary power spike or utility shift.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Ki Regeneration">
    Successfully landing melee hits during a Soul Switch or standard combat now regenerates Ki automatically, encouraging aggressive playstyles.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The Soul Switch mechanic is particularly controversial among purists who prefer traditional transformations like Super Saiyan or Golden Form. However, for races that traditionally lacked diverse transformations—such as Earthlings or a potential Demon race—Soul Switch provides a viable way to stay competitive during high-tier missions.

| Mechanic | Resource Cost | Duration | Primary Benefit |

| **Soul Switch** | Transformation Slot | 40-60 Seconds | Full move set replacement, Stat boost |
