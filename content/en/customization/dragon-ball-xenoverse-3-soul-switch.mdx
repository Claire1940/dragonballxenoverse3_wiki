<Section icon={Swords} title="Soul Switch vs. Soul Assist: Mechanic Comparison">

Understanding the distinction between Soul Switch and Soul Assist is vital for any player looking to optimize their performance in AGE 1000. While both systems involve legendary allies, they serve completely different tactical purposes. Soul Assist is designed for burst damage and combo extensions, allowing an ally to jump in for a singular powerful strike. In contrast, the Soul Switch is a transformative state that alters your hero's fundamental combat behavior for a set duration.

| Feature | Soul Assist | Soul Switch |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Mechanical Goal** | Tag-team combo extension | Temporary character transformation |
| **Duration** | Instantaneous / Single attack | Sustained duration (Meter-based) |
| **Stat Impact** | No change to protagonist stats | Significant buffs based on the Soul |
| **Skill Access** | Ally uses their own skill | Protagonist gains Soul's unique skills |
| **Best Use Case** | Breaking enemy guards or finishing combos | Overcoming elemental or style disadvantages |

The Soul Switch system effectively doubles your tactical options. For example, if you are playing a heavy-hitting Saiyan build but encounter a highly mobile boss, switching to the Soul of a faster character like Future Trunks can provide the agility needed to keep pace. This flexibility is a cornerstone of the new RPG-driven direction Bandai Namco has implemented for the 2027 release.

<Callout type="info" title="Tactical Insight">
Soul Switch is not just about power; it is about utility. Some Souls provide defensive auras or healing properties that your base character might lack, making them essential for long-form survival missions in the West City outskirts.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="How to Master the Soul Switch Flow">

Mastering the Dragon Ball Xenoverse 3 Soul Switch requires more than just pressing a button when the meter is full. To truly dominate the battlefield, you must integrate the switch into your existing combo strings. The transition itself has an active hitbox, meaning a well-timed Soul Switch can actually parry an incoming attack or extend a juggle that would otherwise drop.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Build the Soul Meter">
    Engage in close-quarters combat and land successful Ki attacks to fill the gauge. Successful vanishes and perfect blocks accelerate this process significantly.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Select Your Entry Point">
    Wait for a guard break or a heavy knockback. Activating the Soul Switch during a knockback allows the "Soul" to manifest and continue the pursuit instantly.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Manage the Burn Rate">
    Once active, your meter will drain. Using signature Soul skills will deplete the meter faster. Balance your standard attacks with one or two high-impact Soul abilities.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Execute the Soul Finish">
    Before the meter empties, trigger the Soul Finish. This is a cinematic ultimate attack that consumes the remaining meter but deals massive damage and resets the combat neutral.
  </Step>
</Steps>

</Section>
