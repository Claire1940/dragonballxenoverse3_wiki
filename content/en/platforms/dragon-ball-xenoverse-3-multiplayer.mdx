<Section icon={Shield} title="Combat System & Resource Management">

Combat in **dragon ball xenoverse 3 multiplayer** introduces a fundamental departure from its predecessor's mechanical foundation. The most significant shift is the complete removal of a dedicated stamina bar. Instead, both offensive maneuvers and defensive actions are governed by a single, unified Ki resource. This streamlined system forces players to make critical, split-second decisions during intense skirmishes.

Furthermore, super attacks like Kaioken Assault and the iconic Kamehameha no longer consume Ki. Instead, they operate on individual cooldown timers. This design choice prevents players from spamming high-damage abilities, shifting the meta toward tactical timing and combo preservation.

| Combat Element | Old System (Xenoverse 2) | New System (Xenoverse 3) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Super Attacks** | Consumed Ki resource | **Governed by cooldown timers** |
| **Defensive Escapes** | Consumed Stamina bar | **No dedicated escape slot** |
| **Ki Function** | Exclusively for offensive skills | **Unifies offense and defense** |
| **Resource Caps** | Scaled via character stats | **Homogenized (typically 5 bars)** |

Without a dedicated escape ability slot, players must rely on active blocking, perfect vanishes, and team-based cover fire to break out of enemy combos. Because Ki is a shared resource for both moving and blocking, managing your meter is the difference between maintaining a relentless offensive or being left completely vulnerable.

<Callout type="tip" title="Cooldown Management">
Since super attacks are free but limited by cooldowns, space your abilities out to ensure you always have an offensive option ready to stagger enemies.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Soul Assist & Soul Switch Mechanics">

To enhance the cooperative experience, developers have introduced two groundbreaking mechanics: Soul Assist and Soul Switch. These systems allow players to leverage the power of historic Z Warriors (and potentially iconic villains) to turn the tide of battle.

Soul Assist acts as a summon mechanic. By executing a simple button combination, players can call upon a legendary warrior to perform a brief, high-impact assist attack. Soul Switch, on the other hand, is a temporary, full-character transformation. Activating Soul Switch replaces your character with a hero like Future Trunks for a limited duration (typically around 40 seconds), completely swapping your melee combos, super attacks, and ultimate abilities.

While Soul Switch offers incredible utility and a temporary power spike, players using Saiyan characters may face a strategic dilemma. Because Soul Switch occupies the traditional awakening slot, choosing it means sacrificing iconic transformations like Super Saiyan. Teams must balance their composition to ensure a mix of sustained transformation power and burst-utility character swaps.

<Section icon={Target} title="Boss Battle Strategies & Team Tactics">

Multiplayer boss encounters are highly dynamic, requiring coordination, environmental awareness, and precise role distribution. In battles against colossal threats like Broly (Super), bosses will periodically unleash devastating, arena-wide ultimate attacks. 

During these phases, survival depends on using the environment. For instance, when Broly fires a massive, continuous barrage of ki blasts, players must quickly take cover behind large icebergs scattered across the arena. Standing in the open during these phases typically results in an immediate knockout.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Draw Aggro & Position">
    Have defensive-focused players draw the boss's attention while others position themselves to attack from the flanks.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Deplete Boss Ki">
    Unleash relentless melee combos. Landing physical strikes actively drains the boss's Ki bar, delaying their ability to use ultimate attacks.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Take Environmental Cover">
    When the boss prepares an arena-wide ultimate, immediately stop attacking and seek shelter behind designated environmental obstacles.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Execute Soul Switch Punish">
    Once the boss's ultimate phase ends, activate Soul Switch to deal massive burst damage while the boss is temporarily fatigued.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Another crucial gameplay upgrade is the ability to customize your character's basic melee combos. Players are no longer locked into generic racial attack strings; instead, you can equip entire move sets learned from mentors like Vegeta or Trunks. This allows players to tailor their physical attack speed and reach to better fit their role within the multiplayer squad.

| Boss State | Boss Action | Recommended Team Response |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Neutral Phase** | Standard physical and ki attacks | **Flank, combo, and deplete boss Ki** |
| **Charging Phase** | Preparing arena-wide ultimate | **Disengage and locate cover immediately** |
| **Fatigued Phase** | Vulnerable with depleted Ki | **Unleash Soul Switch and Ultimates** |

<Callout type="warning" title="Melee Priority">
Do not rely solely on ranged ki blasts during boss fights. Melee strikes are the primary method for depleting a boss's Ki bar, making physical engagement mandatory.
</Callout>
